Celebrity Profiling

CLEF @ PAN 2019

Given the english Social Media feed of a celebrity, determine the degree of fame, occupation, age and gender.

Task

Celebrities are among the most prolific users of social media, promoting their personas and rallying followers. This activity is closely tied to genuine writing samples, rendering them worthy research subjects in many respects, not least author profiling.

The Celebrity Profiling task this year is to predict four traits of a celebrity from it's social media communication. The traits are the degree of fame, occupation, age and gender. The social media communication is given as the teaser messages from past Tweets. The goal is to develop a piece of software which predicts celebrity traits from the teaser history.

Corpus [Download Training Data]

The training dataset contains of two files: a feeds.ndjsonexample and a labels.ndjsonexample. Each file lists all celebrities as json objects, one per line and identified by the cid key.

{"cid": 1234, "text": ["a teaser", "another teaser", ...]}
{"cid": 5678, "text": ["a teaser", "another teaser", ...]}
...
feeds.ndjson
{"cid": 1234, "fame": "wellknown", "occupation": "sports", "gender": "female", "age": 28}
{"cid": 5678, "fame": "regional", "occupation": "entertainment", "gender": "male", "age": 54}
...
labels.ndjson
fame        := {regional, wellknown, superstar}
occupation  := {TODO}
age         := {1, ... ,114}
gender      := {male, female, nonbinary}
possible value instances for each label

Evaluation [Download Evaluation Script]

For evaluation, your software will read a feeds.ndjson file from a given directory and write a valid labels.ndjson with your predictions to a given output directory: 

mySoftware -i path/to/feeds/directory -o path/to/output/directory

Submissions are judged by a combined metric cRank, which is the harmonic mean of each label's metric. Fame, occupation, and gender are judged by their respective F1. Age is judged by Mean Squared Error.

$$ \text{cRank} = {4 \over {\frac{1}{\text{F}_{1, \text{fame}}} + \frac{1}{\text{F}_{1, \text{occupation}}} + \frac{1}{\text{F}_{1, \text{gender}}} + \frac{1}{\text{MSE}_{\text{age}}}}} $$

Submission [See the TIRA User Guide]

When you registered, you will get remote access to a virtual machine (Windows or Linux) to deploy the task software in. Your software must be executable from the command line and not require Internet access during the evaluation period. Note that you retain full copyrights of your software, but agree to grant us usage rights only for the purpose of the competition.
